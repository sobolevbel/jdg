# Pension: emerytura

The Polish pension is defined-contribution in spirit: whatever pension
contributions you actually paid over your lifetime is what you get back,
stretched over your statistical life expectancy. No "points" and no salary
averaging — just your personal account at ZUS.

## How the pension is calculated

The formula from [art. 26 of the pension act][1]:

    PENSION = ACCUMULATED CAPITAL / AVERAGE LIFE EXPECTANCY IN MONTHS

**Capital** is the sum of all pension contributions you paid (składka emerytalna,
19.52% of the base). The capital is indexed every year (waloryzacja) — in recent
years indexation has been high (around 9–15% a year), so old contributions don't
melt away with inflation. If you worked in Poland before 1999, a kapitał
początkowy is added on top.

**Life expectancy** is the average expected number of months of life after
retirement according to the [GUS table][2]. The table is published every spring
and applies from 1 April to 31 March: per the 2026/27 table it is 222.7 months
at age 65 and 268.9 months at age 60.

!!! example "Illustrative calculation (2026 prices, no indexation)"
    A man paid the minimum Duży ZUS for 25 years: `1,103.27 zł × 12 × 25 ≈ 331,000 zł`
    of capital. Pension: `331,000 / 222.7 ≈ 1,490 zł` gross — **below the minimum**.
    With a 25-year record it gets topped up to the minimum pension (see below).
    The real amount will be higher thanks to yearly indexation of the capital,
    but the order of magnitude holds: minimum contributions → minimum pension.

Three takeaways follow from the formula:

1. The **higher** your contribution base and the **longer** you pay, the bigger
   the pension.
2. Every year worked past the retirement age increases the pension noticeably:
   the capital grows while the divisor (life expectancy) shrinks.
3. With the same contributions, women's pensions are markedly lower: retirement
   5 years earlier (less capital) and a bigger divisor (268.9 vs 222.7).

## Retirement age and applying

The retirement age is **60 for women and 65 for men**. The pension is not granted
automatically: you file an **EMP** application (easiest via [eZUS](zus_login.md)),
no earlier than 30 days before reaching the age.

You do **not** have to close your JDG: a retired entrepreneur may run the business
with no income limits, and of the contributions only the składka zdrowotna
remains due — składki społeczne become voluntary.

The pension is subject to income tax (PIT) and the 9% health contribution.

## The minimum pension

From 1 March 2026 the minimum pension is **1,978.49 zł gross** (~1,800 net);
the amount is indexed every March ([current figure][3]). If your formula-based
pension comes out lower, it is topped up to the minimum — but only with a
sufficient **insurance record** ([art. 87][4]):

- **20 years** for women;
- **25 years** for men.

The record = okresy składkowe + nieskładkowe:

- **składkowe** — months for which pension contributions were paid: employment,
  JDG (including on Preferencyjne and Mały ZUS Plus), and periods of receiving
  [zasiłek macierzyński](zus_chorobowe.md) — during maternity leave the state
  budget pays your contributions;
- **nieskładkowe** — for example sick leave and university studies; they count
  only up to **1/3 of the proven składkowe periods** ([art. 5][5]);
- months on **Ulga na Start** yield neither record nor capital — there simply is
  no pension insurance during that period.

If the record is insufficient, the pension is paid strictly by the formula —
even 50 złoty (the so-called "groszowe emerytury"). There is no minimum record
for a pension as such: even one month of contributions entitles you to a (tiny)
payout once you reach the age.

## What shrinks an entrepreneur's pension

Every month on ZUS reliefs means less capital (2026 numbers):

| Mode | Składka emerytalna per month | Into capital per year |
|---|---|---|
| Ulga na Start | 0 zł (no insurance) | 0 zł |
| Składki preferencyjne | 281.44 zł | ~3,377 zł |
| Duży ZUS (minimum) | 1,103.27 zł | ~13,239 zł |

Reliefs are effectively a loan from your own old age: you save on contributions
now and receive less later. Not a reason to skip them — but a reason to know
their price.

## How to increase your future pension

1. **Pay contributions on a higher base.** An entrepreneur may declare a
   contribution base above the minimum — up to the yearly 30-krotność cap
   (282,600 zł in 2026, [details][6]). Note that the other składki społeczne are
   calculated from the same base, not just the pension one.
2. **Private retirement accounts** — more flexible and fully inheritable:
    - **IKZE**: the 2026 limit for JDG is elevated — **16,956 zł** (11,304 zł for
      employees); contributions are **deducted from your PIT base** — the state
      effectively refunds part of the contribution right away; at payout after
      65 only a flat 10% tax applies;
    - **IKE**: limit 28,260 zł; no deduction, but the payout after 60 is entirely
      tax-free (including no capital gains "Belka" tax);
    - current limits at [ike-ikze.pl][7].
3. **Work longer.** Postponing retirement is the most powerful multiplier:
   more capital and a smaller divisor.

## What is inherited and what is not

The 19.52% pension contribution is split in two: 12.22 p.p. go to your main ZUS
account — that money is **not inheritable**. The remaining 7.3 p.p. go to the ZUS
**subkonto** (or partly to OFE if you are a member) — subkonto and OFE funds
**are inheritable**. IKE and IKZE are inherited in full.

## How to check your pension capital

In [eZUS](zus_login.md), the Ubezpieczony panel offers the **Informacja o stanie
konta ubezpieczonego** — how much capital you have accumulated and how it has
been indexed. There is also a **kalkulator emerytalny** that forecasts your
pension from your data.

## If you leave Poland

A pension earned in Poland is not lost:

- **EU/EEA countries**: social security coordination — insurance periods are
  summed and the pension is transferred to your country of residence.
- **Countries with a bilateral agreement** ([ZUS list][8]): including
  **Ukraine** (in force since 2014, [text][9]) and **Belarus** (in force since
  01.04.2022, [text][10]) — insurance periods in both countries are summed for
  pension entitlement, and a granted pension is transferred to the country of
  residence.
- **Russia and other countries without an agreement**: periods are not summed;
  the Polish pension for Polish periods is still due, but ZUS does not transfer
  it to such countries — it is paid to a Polish bank account (or to an
  authorized person).
